Ah, "ascetic"! Let's explore the concept of self-discipline and minimalistic living with this word!

Usage: When someone is described as an "ascetic," it's like they have taken a vow to live a life of simplicity and self-denial—it refers to a person who practices extreme self-discipline, often abstaining from worldly pleasures and leading a life of austerity.

Etymology: The word "ascetic" comes from the Greek word "askēsis," which means "exercise" or "training." It's like describing someone who trains themselves to detach from material desires and find inner peace through a minimalist lifestyle.
