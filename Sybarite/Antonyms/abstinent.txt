Ah, "abstinent"! Let's explore the concept of self-restraint and avoiding indulgence with this word!

Usage: When someone is described as "abstinent," it's like they have a superpower to resist temptations—it refers to a person who voluntarily refrains from certain activities, particularly those involving pleasure or desire, often for moral, religious, or health reasons.

Etymology: The word "abstinent" comes from the Latin word "abstinēre," which means "to hold back" or "to refrain." It's like describing someone who exercises self-control and chooses to deny themselves certain pleasures or behaviors.
